North Indian Cuisines

  • Punjab

    1. Butter Chicken

      Butter chicken is a globally popular Indian dish. It features pieces of tender chicken, sometimes with a smoky flavor from a clay oven. The chicken is cooked in a smooth, creamy tomato sauce. The sauce is made rich with butter and cream. Spices like garam masala, cumin, and coriander give it a warm, complex taste. Dried fenugreek leaves add a unique aroma. A touch of sugar or honey balances the tomato's tang. It's often served with warm naan bread or fluffy rice. The dish is known for its mild, comforting flavors. It's a popular choice in Indian restaurants around the world.

    2. Dal Makhani

      Dal makhani is a rich and creamy Indian lentil dish. It features whole black lentils and red kidney beans. The dish includes butter and cream for a smooth, luxurious texture. Spices like garam masala, ginger, and garlic add a warm, complex flavor. It's slow-cooked for many hours, which makes it extra creamy. Tomatoes give it a slight tang. Dried fenugreek leaves add a unique aroma. It's often served with rice or naan bread. Dal makhani is known for its comforting, hearty taste.

  • Uttar Pradesh

    1. Awadh Biriyani

      Awadhi biryani is a fragrant rice dish from Lucknow, India. It includes long-grain basmati rice, tender pieces of marinated meat (usually lamb or chicken), and a blend of aromatic spices. The dish features saffron for a golden color and delicate flavor. It uses rosewater and kewra water for a unique, sweet aroma. Ghee (clarified butter) adds richness. Fried onions provide a sweet, crispy texture. It often contains dried fruits like plums and raisins. Awadhi biryani is known for its mild, subtle flavors and delicate spices.

    2. Tunday Kebab

      Tunday kebab is a famous dish from Lucknow, India. It's made with very finely minced buffalo meat. The meat is mixed with a special blend of over 160 spices. These spices include things like cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and many others kept secret. Papaya is added to tenderize the meat. Ghee (clarified butter) is used to cook the kebabs. They are very soft and melt in your mouth. Tunday kebabs are known for their delicate texture and complex, aromatic flavor. They are usually served with paratha, a type of flatbread, or rumali roti, a thin handkerchief bread.

  • Rajasthan

    1. Ker Sangri

      Ker sangri is a traditional Rajasthani dish known for its unique flavors. It features dried ker berries and sangri beans. The dish includes spices like cumin, coriander, and chili powder for a warm, spicy taste. It often uses dried mango powder (amchur) for a tangy flavor. It's cooked with mustard oil, which adds a distinct pungent taste. Sometimes, it includes dried raisins or nuts for a touch of sweetness and texture. It's a dry vegetable dish, not a curry. Ker sangri is often served with roti or bajra roti. It's a staple in desert areas due to the plants' ability to survive in harsh conditions.

    2. Dal Bhaati Churma

      Dal baati churma is a traditional Rajasthani dish with three main parts: baati, dal, and churma. Baati are hard, round wheat breads cooked over coals or in an oven. Dal is a lentil soup, often made with a mix of lentils and spices. Churma is a sweet, crumbly dessert made from wheat flour, ghee, and sugar or jaggery. The baati are dipped in the dal. The churma is eaten as a sweet side. Ghee is used generously in all parts of the dish. It has a hearty, rustic, and sweet taste.

  • Kashmir

    1. Rogan Josh

      Rogan Josh is a flavorful lamb curry from Kashmir. It features tender pieces of lamb. The dish uses a rich, red sauce. The red color comes from Kashmiri chili powder and alkanet flower, which gives it a deep hue. Spices like fennel seeds, ginger, and cardamom add warmth. Yogurt is sometimes used to thicken the sauce. The dish is slow-cooked, making the lamb very tender. It's known for its aromatic and relatively mild spicy flavor. It's often served with rice or naan bread.

    2. Modur Pulao

      Modur pulao is a sweet and aromatic rice dish from Kashmiri cuisine. It features long-grain rice, often basmati. The dish includes ghee, which adds richness. It has dry fruits like almonds, cashews, and raisins for sweetness and texture. Spices like saffron, cardamom, and cinnamon give it a warm, fragrant aroma. Sugar or honey is used to sweeten the dish. It's often garnished with silver leaf. Modur pulao is known for its sweet, festive flavor and is often served during special occasions.
